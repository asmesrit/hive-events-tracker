1. What is HIVE?

HIVE is our college's portal for tracking every hackathon, workshop, contest and event you participate in. Instead of losing track of registrations across many websites, you log each participation once in HIVE and update its status as it progresses — and your whole team sees it too. Faculty get a live view of all student participation, with search, analytics and downloadable reports.

Part A — For Students

2. Creating your account

  1. Open https://srit-hive.web.app in any browser (phone or laptop).
  2. Click Create your account.
  3. Choose Student.
  4. Enter your college email — it must end with @sritcbe.ac.in. Personal emails will not be accepted here.
  5. Set a password (minimum 6 characters), or tick "Use the default password" to use the standard one. You can change it anytime later from your Profile.
  6. Click Continue and fill in your profile: full name, year, department, register number, and your top skills (type a skill and press Enter to add each one). You can also add extra info like your GitHub or LinkedIn, one per line as Label: value.
  7. Click Create account — you land straight on your dashboard. 🎉
Tip: If a teammate already added you to an event using your college email before you registered, that event appears in your account automatically the moment you sign up.

3. Logging in (two ways)

Sign in at https://srit-hive.web.app with your college email + password.

Optional: link your personal email

  1. Go to Profile → Login emails.
  2. Enter your personal email (e.g. Gmail) and click Link.
  3. From now on you can sign in with either email — the password is the same one.

4. Adding an event you're participating in

  1. Click ＋ Add event (dashboard or My Events).
  2. Event name — start typing. If the event was already posted by faculty or entered by another team, it appears in a dropdown — select it from the list so faculty can group all participants of the same event together. If it's not there, just type the full name.
  3. Event type — Hackathon, Workshop, Paper Presentation… choose Other to type your own.
  4. Team size — total members including you.
  5. Team members — add everyone except yourself (you're included automatically). Three cases:
    Your teammate is…What to doWhat happens
    Registered on HIVEChoose Registered on HIVE and search their nameThe event instantly appears in their My Events too
    SRIT student, not on HIVE yetChoose SRIT student, enter their name + @sritcbe.ac.in emailThe event auto-attaches to their account when they register
    From another collegeChoose Other college, enter name + personal emailStored for records only
  6. Dates to track — add labelled dates like "Round 1", "Submission deadline", "Finals". These show up as reminders on your dashboard.
  7. Current status — free text describing where you are ("Round 2 cleared, waiting for shortlist").
  8. Overall status — active while ongoing; change to won or lost when it concludes.
  9. Click Add event.
Keep it updated! Open any event → use Quick status update whenever something changes. Faculty reports rely on your statuses being current.

5. Your dashboard

  • Stat cards — total events, active, won (with win rate), lost.
  • Charts — split by event type, outcomes, and your participation over time.
  • ⏰ Upcoming dates — every tracked date across all your events, nearest first.

6. Opportunities — find & share events

  • The Opportunities page lists events posted by faculty (📢 broadcasts) and by fellow students (💡).
  • Found an interesting hackathon somewhere? Click ＋ Post an event and share the name, registration link and notes — everyone can see it.
  • Want to participate in a posted event? Click 🎯 Track this event — the add-event form opens pre-filled and linked to that posting. Fill in your team and save.

7. Managing your profile

  • Profile — update name, year, department, register number, skills any time.
  • Change password — enter your current password and a new one (also works if you kept the default password and want your own).

Part B — For Faculty / Admin

8. Creating a faculty account

  1. Get a faculty invite code from the admin team (each code works exactly once).
  2. Open https://srit-hive.web.app → Create your account → choose Faculty / Admin.
  3. Enter the invite code, your @sritcbe.ac.in email, a password, and your profile details.
  4. After signing in you'll see the extra Admin section in the sidebar: Analytics, Students, All Events, Reports.
Need codes for colleagues? Go to Admin → Reports → Faculty invite codes, type a new code and click Create code. Share it privately — one code, one account.

9. College analytics (Admin → Analytics)

  • Live totals: students registered, participation entries, wins, losses.
  • Charts: participations by event type, outcome split, department-wise comparison, 12-month trend.
  • 🏅 Most active students — click any row to open that student's full profile.

10. Students & master search (Admin → Students)

  • Search by name, register number or email; filter by department and year.
  • View opens the student's page: profile, skills, personal alias, and their complete participation history with statuses.
  • Edit lets you correct a student's details directly in the interface; Delete removes their HIVE profile.

11. All events & filtering participants (Admin → All Events)

  • Every participation entry across the college, in one table.
  • Filter by event name (see all teams registered under one event), status, type, department, and a from/to date range.
  • The 🔗 icon means the entry is linked to a posted opportunity.
  • Open any entry to view details, update its status, or delete it.

12. Reports & notifying students (Admin → Reports / Opportunities)

Generating reports

  1. Pick a preset: All participations · Events won · Currently active · Events lost.
  2. Optionally narrow by event type, department, and date period.
  3. Preview updates instantly. Click ⬇ CSV (for Excel) or ⬇ PDF (formatted document).

Notifying students about an event

  1. Go to Opportunities → ＋ Post an event.
  2. Enter the event name, type, registration link and notes; keep 📢 Broadcast to all students ticked.
  3. Students see it in their Opportunities feed and can register for tracking with one click — their entries stay linked to your post, so you can filter exactly who signed up under it.

13. Troubleshooting & FAQ

Problem / questionAnswer
"Only @sritcbe.ac.in email addresses can register"Account creation needs your college email. Link your personal email later from Profile.
I forgot my passwordContact the admin team — they can reset it from the Firebase console. If you know it, change it yourself in Profile.
Can I log in with my personal email?Yes — after linking it in Profile → Login emails. Same password for both.
I added a teammate but they can't see the eventIf they're registered, add them via Registered on HIVE (name search). If added by email, they'll see it after they register with that exact college email.
My event doesn't appear in the name dropdownThe dropdown shows already-posted/tracked events. If yours is new, just type the full name — the next team will then find it in the dropdown.
Who can edit or delete an event entry?Any team member can update it; the creator and faculty can delete it.
"Invalid or already-used faculty invite code"Each code works once. Ask an existing faculty member to mint a new one (Admin → Reports).
The page looks stuck or outdatedRefresh the browser (Ctrl+R). HIVE is a web app — no installation or updates needed.
