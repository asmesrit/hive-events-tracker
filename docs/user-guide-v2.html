1. What is HIVE?

HIVE is our college's portal for tracking every hackathon, workshop, contest and event you participate in. Log each participation once, keep its status updated, attach your certificates and photos — and your whole team (and mentors) see it too. Faculty get live analytics, search, mentoring views and downloadable reports.

✨ What's new in version 2

  • 📜 Certificates — upload participation/winner certificates; stored in the college Drive
  • 📸 Event photos — multiple photos with previews & progress (optional; skip for virtual events)
  • 💰 Prize money — record the amount and currency you won
  • 🧑‍🏫 Faculty mentors — add one or more mentors to any participation
  • ⏰ Deadline reminders — a popup at login when a posted event's deadline is today/tomorrow
  • 🗓️ Post expiry — posted events show a "valid till" date and mark themselves Expired
  • 📄 Student reports — faculty download per-student PDFs; students download their own profile report
  • 📱 Better mobile view — the whole portal is now phone-friendly

Part A — For Students

2. Creating your account & logging in

  1. Open https://srit-hive.web.app → Create your account → choose Student.
  2. Use your college email ending with @sritcbe.ac.in (personal emails are rejected here).
  3. Set a password (min 6 characters) or tick "Use the default password". Change it anytime in Profile.
  4. Fill your profile: name, year, department, register number, skills (press Enter after each), and optional extras (GitHub: handle, one per line).

Two ways to sign in

Link your personal email in Profile → Login emails — then sign in with either email using the same password.

Joined late? If a teammate added you to an event (or a mentor role) using your college email before you registered, it attaches to your account automatically the moment you sign up.

3. Adding an event

  1. Click ＋ Add event.
  2. Event name — start typing; select from the dropdown if the event was already posted or tracked by another team (this groups all participants for faculty). Otherwise type the full name.
  3. Event type — pick one, or Other to type your own.
  4. Team size — total members including you.
  5. Received prize money? NEW — choose Yes to reveal Amount and Currency (INR / USD / EUR / Other → specify).
  6. Team members — add everyone except yourself:
    Teammate is…What to doWhat happens
    Registered on HIVE (student or faculty)Search their name, click to addEvent instantly appears in their My Events
    SRIT student/faculty, not on HIVE yetEnter name + @sritcbe.ac.in emailAuto-attaches when they register
    From another collegeEnter name + personal emailStored for records
  7. Faculty mentors NEW — optional, add one or more: search registered faculty by name/email and click ＋ Add mentor, or use "Not registered yet" with their official SRIT mail. Mentors see your team in their My Mentoring page.
  8. Dates to track — labelled dates ("Round 1", "Submission deadline") that appear as dashboard reminders.
  9. Status — free-text progress + overall active / won / lost.
  10. Click Add event — you'll get a success message and land on My Events with the new entry at the top.

4. Certificates 📜 NEW

  1. Open your event → 📜 Certificates card → ⬆ Upload certificate.
  2. A Google Drive uploader opens in a new tab (sign in with your SRIT Google account).
  3. Choose the type (Participation / Winner / Other), a label, and the file (JPG/PNG/WEBP/PDF, max 10 MB). You can upload as many as you need, one after another.
  4. Return to HIVE → the certificate appears automatically (or press 🔄 Check for new uploads).

Certificates are stored in the college Drive; you and faculty can open them anytime with the View ↗ link.

5. Event photos 📸 NEW

Add photos of you participating — on stage, presenting, receiving the award. Optional: skip it for virtual/online events.

  1. Open your event → 📸 Event photos card → 📸 Upload photos.
  2. In the uploader tab, pick the Event photos tab → select multiple images at once → previews appear before you upload.
  3. Click ⬆ Upload — a progress bar shows each file going up ("Uploading 2 of 3…").
  4. Back in HIVE, the photos show as a thumbnail gallery on the event page — click any to view full size.

6. Your dashboard

  • Stat cards: total events, active, won (with win rate), lost.
  • Charts: by event type, outcomes, participation over time.
  • ⏰ Upcoming dates — all tracked dates across your events, nearest first.

7. Opportunities & deadline reminders ⏰

  • The Opportunities feed lists events posted by faculty (📢) and students (💡).
  • Posting an event? Add a "Valid till" date NEW (registration deadline or event date). After that date the post shows an ⏳ Expired badge — it stays for reference, and the filter lets you view Live / Expired / Everything.
  • Deadline popup NEW — if a posted event's deadline is today or tomorrow when you log in, HIVE pops up a reminder with a 🎯 Track button (shown once per day).
  • 🎯 Track this event — one click opens the add-event form pre-linked to the posting.

8. My Events & keeping things updated

  • Search + filter your events by name, status, and type. The 📜 icon marks entries with certificates.
  • Open any event → Quick status update to change the progress or mark it won/lost — keep it current, faculty reports depend on it!

9. Profile & your personal report 📄 NEW

  • Profile — update details, skills, link your personal email, change your password.
  • ⬇ Download my profile report — one click generates a detailed PDF of you: profile info, participation stats, every event with mentors & prize money, and all certificate links. Perfect for sharing with your department or attaching to applications.

Part B — For Faculty / Admin

10. Faculty account & invite codes

  1. Get a one-time invite code from the admin team.
  2. Register at https://srit-hive.web.app → Faculty / Admin → enter the code + your SRIT email.
  3. The Admin section appears in your sidebar: Analytics, Students, All Events, My Mentoring, Reports.
  4. Mint codes for colleagues at Admin → Reports → Faculty invite codes.

11. My Mentoring 🧑‍🏫 NEW

  • Every team that names you as a faculty mentor appears here — with stat tiles (teams / active / won / lost), search and status filters.
  • Click any row to open the full event: team, statuses, certificates, photos.
  • ⬇ CSV / ⬇ PDF — export a report of just your mentored teams (includes prize money and certificate links).
  • If a student added you as mentor before you registered, those teams attach to you automatically at signup.

12. Students, search & student reports 📄 NEW

  • Admin → Students — search by name/register number/email; filter by department and year; edit or delete profiles in place.
  • Open a student → full profile, participation history, and a consolidated 📜 Certificates list with links.
  • ⬇ Student report (PDF) — a formatted per-student document: profile header, stat chips, participation table (with mentors & prize money), and certificate links. ⬇ CSV for spreadsheets. Ready to forward to the department.

13. All Events & analytics

  • All Events — every participation across the college; filter by event, status, type, department and date range. Icons: 🔗 linked to a posting · 📜 has certificates.
  • Analytics — college-wide totals, event-type split, outcomes, department comparison, 12-month trend, most-active students.

14. Reports (report builder)

  1. Admin → Reports → choose a preset: All / Won / Active / Lost.
  2. Narrow by type, department, and period — the preview updates live.
  3. ⬇ CSV or ⬇ PDF. Reports now include NEW: Faculty mentor(s), Prize money, Photos count, Certificates count and Certificate links — anyone with the report can open the certificates via the links.

15. Posting & notifying with deadlines

  1. Opportunities → ＋ Post an event — name, type, registration link, notes, and the Valid till date; keep 📢 Broadcast ticked.
  2. Students get the feed entry, and on the deadline day HIVE also pops up a reminder at login for everyone.
  3. After the date passes the post marks itself ⏳ Expired (kept for records, filterable) — no manual cleanup needed.
  4. Entries created via 🎯 Track stay linked to your post, so you can filter exactly who registered under it.
